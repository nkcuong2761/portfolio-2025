GoodHood.SG: Designing for Connected Communities

Product Design, UX Research, Mobile

I joined GoodHood.SG in May 2023 as a Product Designer, embarking on a journey to transform the digital neighborhood hub. From conducting research and refining UX to crafting compelling data visualizations and prototypes, I had the privilege of leading a comprehensive UI overhaul for our mobile apps. The result? The most significant update on GoodHood was released in August 2023.

My Role

Product Designer

Timeline

since 05/2023

Tools

Figma, Mixpanel, Google Suite

— Overview —

GoodHood.SG, a Singapore-based digital neighborhood hub affectionately known as a 'kampung,' aims to unite neighbors for a vibrant and close-knit community. Our vision centers around fostering local connections through neighborhood feeds and chats while promoting sustainability through free giving and receiving initiatives among Singaporeans.

Timeline

Taking advantage of the patriotic spirit that sweeps through Singapore on its National Day every August 9th, our team strategically chose this period to promote our product. Our goal was clear: attract new users, boost Daily Active Users (DAU), and enhance store ratings.

— Challenge & Goal —

In early June 2023, the app had around 2052 Total DAU, with an average of 19 new users per day. Recognizing the need for a fresh look and a rebrand to appeal to a broader audience, my initial task was to revamp the product comprehensively. The UX of our selling features needed attention to impact conversion and retention rates positively.

Business Selling Points
  • Our platform offered a neighborhood-exclusive feed where neighbors could share stories, events, and notices, and join interest groups. Moreover, we provided a space for neighbors to give away items they no longer needed.

“One man's trash is another man's treasure” — except that we don't call it “trash”; it has a bad notation to it haha.

Would our selling points change? Until now I can say that they have. But at the end of the day, every startup has to adapt to the market quickly to thrive. I realized this soon and began a journey to align our business goals with users' insights and needs.

— Discovery —

Understanding the evolving market, I conducted user interviews and analyzed data through tools like Mixpanel.

User Interviews

While most of my work was remote from Vietnam, I had the opportunity to visit Singapore and attend a volunteer workshop of GoodHood. I had the chance to interview our beloved volunteers and to learn from the power users of our product.

Analytics — Mixpanel

Embracing the power of numbers, I delved into data analytics using Mixpanel. Though challenging initially, mastering this tool opened up opportunities for in-depth UX research.

Findings and Strategy

After engaging with power users in GoodHood and collecting new data, I uncovered insights that guided my decision-making:

  • High Usage of Free Giving System: The Free Giving system emerged as the most frequently used feature.
  • Central Role of the Free Page: The majority of app interactions occurred on the Free page.
  • Neighborhood Feed Dominance: Among the four content platforms (neighborhood feed, events, notices, groups), the neighborhood feed garnered the most engagement. Groups, however, saw the least engagement, despite the total number of posts being relatively low.

“We have to move quick, so let's prioritize”.

Realizing that our biggest selling point has shifted to the Free Giving system, we made a finite decision to focus on improving the UX of this feature.

  • Interactions and engagement are high for Free items (High Demand)
  • The number of listings is low (Low Supply)
  • The number of posts created is low (Low Supply + Low Incentive)

— Focusing on Free Giving —

Conducting a UX audit of the old GoodHood.SG app (April 2023 and earlier), I identified areas for improvement.

“Free” Discovery

After the onboarding process, new users will land on this home page. There are a few issues on this screen:

  • The route to the Free page was tucked inside a non-sticky menu on the home screen
    • Down-prioritize the feature
    • Hard to access if users are on a different screen.
“Free” Listings Feed
  • A huge “nudge to list” section pushes the main content of the page down.
  • The “Like”, or “Heart” icon, is put inside the image frame → increases the chance of user error where a person could end up enlarging the image instead of liking it.
  • A quick filter includes the “Availability” option, which allows users to see “Gone” items — those that had been given away.
    • Why would they want to do that?
    • A flawed function. There's a better way to make use of this.
  • The “No result” search page is empty. Opportunities arise here!
Creating a “Free” Listings — Benchmarking Competitors

During the audit process, I did numerous iterations of benchmarking direct competitors. These competitors are the ones that have a very similar feature to ours, and in this scenario, we'll look at Olio's UX for creating a Free Listing. This gave us insights on what a good solution looks like, and how to minimize steps for ease of conversion.

(Benchmarking is not copying. It's a way of learning while not re-inventing the wheel at the same time, saving resources for small startups like us who have a very tight timeline)

Receiving a Request

If someone requests a Free item from the Giver, a request message will appear in the Giver's chat list. This flow seems simple, but there are a few hiccups:

  • No differentiation between “Set as recipient” and “Leave a review” CTA
  • “Set as recipient” is also hard to understand for new users

→ The process can be hard to follow through for new users → high drop-off rate

The goal was to make the process of Creating & Requesting Free items easier & more transparent for Givers and Takers.

— Iterating & Validating Assumptions —

Through iterative feedback loops and weekly design critiques, I embarked on a 2-month sprint for the rebrand, aligning with the marketing push before the Singapore National Day Parade. Key goals and planning included:

  • Task Management: Utilizing tools like Trello for task distribution and sprint planning.
  • UX Copy: Recognizing the importance of comprehensive designs, I invested time in perfecting UX copy.
  • "Free" now has its dedicated page.
  • "My listing" navigation for quick access.
  • Improved placement of the "Heart" icon.
  • Added a "Share" option to enhance the giving process.
  • Redesigned the "Plus" icon in the bottom nav for a direct listing creation.
  • Sticky Quick Filter on top to improve browsing experience
  • Add a “Top Gone items” section to invoke a sense of FOMO within users → push potential free-takers to keep going back into the app to never miss out on hot listings.

No Result Search State:

  • Encouraged users to refer more people to join GoodHood.SG for added incentive
  • Displayed a list of "Nearby items" to extend user journey.
Creating Free Listing
  • Constraint the process of Creating a Free Listing within one page for simplicity.
  • Pre-fill “Pick up details” from last created post to save time for frequent Givers.
  • Sticky “List Now” CTA at the bottom for ease of reachability as well as better highlight for when all inputs are sufficient.
New Process of Giving Items
  • A clearer copy conveys the value of the action.
  • Dynamic "State" updates on top of the chat page.
  • Sticky CTA box at the top of the keyboard as a constant reminder.
  • Customized "System message" guiding users on recent events and next steps.

Why stop there? Cease the opportunity!

Post a successful arrangement, users are prompted to give a 5-star review and are immediately nudged to rate the app on the App Store/Google Play. This strategic move aligns with our goal of turning satisfied users into positive reviewers, contributing to increased app ratings.

The results? Exactly what we wanted!

— Results —

This case study will get extremely long if I cover every change during the re-branding. Through just a glimpse, I would still want to show a quick review of the big update.

Impact

While this overview provides a glimpse of our significant update, the impact has been substantial. We observed a rise in app ratings and received positive feedback from users nationwide. Notably, there were significant increases in DAU and Free metrics recorded through backend analytics.

My journey at GoodHood.SG is a testament to the power of adaptability, user-centric design, and a commitment to creating a thriving community through digital connections.
